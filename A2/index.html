The Eight-Thousanders

These visualisations will discuss and visualise data surrounding the world's 14 highest peaks, all exceeding 8000 metres in height. They are commonly referred to as The Eight-Thousanders

Location and Altitude of the Eight-Thousanders

We can see that all 14 peaks lie across the Himalayan and Karakoram ranges, spanning across the border regions of Pakistan, India, Nepal, and China. 8 of the 14 peaks lie in Nepal: These include Mt. Everest, Kangchenjunga, Lhotse, Makalu, Cho Oyu, Dhaulagiri I, Manaslu, and Annapurna I. Pakistan holds K2, Nanga Parbat, Gasherbrum I and II, and Broad Peak. China is home to Shishipangma, the lowest of the 14 peaks, with an altitude of 8,034 metres.


Nations who've conquered the Eight-Thousanders

Only 44 people who have conquered all 14 Eight-Thousanders. We can see the distribution of where these climbers have come from in this visualisation.


Costs of climbing the Eight-Thousanders

Ranging up to approximately $40,000 USD, climbing and conquering the Eight-Thousanders is no cheap feat. For one person to climb all 14 peaks, their individual cost would be upwards of $265,000
